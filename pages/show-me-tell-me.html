Show Me, Tell Me Questions

Master the DVSA vehicle safety questions before your practical test. Learn exactly what the examiner will ask and how to answer confidently.

Tell Me

Q: Tell me how you'd check that the brakes are working before starting a journey.

A: Brakes should not feel spongy or slack. Brakes should be tested as you set off. Vehicle should not pull to one side.

Tell Me

Q: Tell me where you'd find the info for the recommended tyre pressures for this car.

A: You can find this in the manufacturer's guide, or sometimes on a sticker inside the driver's door shut, or inside the fuel filler flap.

Tell Me

Q: Tell me how you make sure your head restraint is correctly adjusted.

A: The head restraint should be adjusted so the rigid part of the head restraint is at least as high as the eye or top of the ears, and as close to the back of the head as is comfortable.

Show Me

Q: Show me how you'd check that the horn is working.

A: Turn on ignition if necessary. Check is carried out by pressing the horn (or center of steering wheel) and listening for the sound.

Show Me

Q: Show me how you'd clean the windscreen using the washers and wipers.

A: Turn on ignition if necessary. Operate the right-hand stalk (usually by pulling it towards you) to spray washer fluid and activate wipers.

Show Me

Q: Show me how you'd open the window and safely close it again.

A: Operate the window control button on the door panel to fully open and then fully close the window.

Tell Me

Q: Tell me how you would check that the headlights and tail lights are working.

A: Explain you'd operate the switch (turn on ignition if necessary), then walk round vehicle to check the lights are on.

Tell Me

Q: Tell me how you would know if there was a problem with your anti-lock braking system.

A: Warning light should illuminate if there is a fault with the anti-lock braking system.

Show Me

Q: Show me how you'd switch on your dipped headlights and then switch to main beam.

A: Turn on ignition or start engine if necessary. Operate the light switch to dipped headlights, then switch to main beam. Check the main beam warning light is on.
